# v15 Tranche 1 — Brand Identity (name, logo, color system)

> **For agentic workers:** REQUIRED SUB-SKILL: superpowers:subagent-driven-development (recommended)
> or superpowers:executing-plans. Steps use checkbox (`- [ ]`) syntax.
>
> This tranche is **docs + assets only** — no package/token/code rename happens here. It establishes
> the brand so T2 can introduce `--cascivo-brand-*` tokens against a decided palette and the apps can
> consume a real logo.
>
> Current-state assumptions — RE-VERIFY before starting:
>
> - `docs/BRAND.md` exists and currently reads as a domain-search log recommending **Cascivo**
>   (`cascivo.com`). The owner has purchased `cascivo.com`.
> - There is no logo asset and no brand color palette in the repo yet.
> - The ten component themes live in `packages/themes/src/*.css` and are neutral/utility — the brand
>   palette is **separate** from them.

**Goal:** Decide and document the brand: the name story (`cascade-ui → cascivo`), a logo proposal
(in-repo SVG), and a brand color palette with contrast verification. Output is a rewritten
`docs/BRAND.md`, a logo SVG, and a brand-color spec that T2 will turn into tokens.

**Architecture:** Markdown + one or two SVG assets. The brand palette is specified in oklch here and
implemented as `--cascivo-brand-*` tokens in T2. Nothing in `packages/components` consumes brand
tokens — they are for the apps/logo/OG only.

**Tech stack:** unchanged.

### Task 2: Brand color palette + contrast

**Files:**

- Modify: `docs/BRAND.md` (palette section)
- Create: `docs/specs/brand-color.md` (detailed palette spec for T2 to implement)

- [ ] **Step 1:** Define the brand palette (distinct from the 10 neutral component themes). Propose:
  - **Primary — cascade blue:** `oklch(0.55 0.15 240)` (≈ `#2563d6`-ish confident azure; water/flow)
  - **Accent — flow teal:** `oklch(0.72 0.13 195)` (energy, the "alive" in `-ivo`)
  - **Ink:** `oklch(0.22 0.03 250)` (near-black with a cool cast, for wordmark on light)
  - **Paper:** `oklch(0.99 0.005 250)` (off-white brand surface)
  - **Brand gradient:** primary → accent (used in hero, logo fill option, OG).
- [ ] **Step 2:** Verify contrast for every intended pairing: wordmark ink on paper, primary on
      paper, white on primary (button), accent on ink. Use the v14 contrast utility
      (`scripts/checks/color/contrast.ts`) if present; record each ratio and whether it meets WCAG AA
      (4.5:1 text / 3:1 large/UI). Adjust lightness until each intended pairing passes; pin final
      oklch values.
- [ ] **Step 3:** Write `docs/specs/brand-color.md`: final tokens table
      (`--cascivo-brand-primary`, `-accent`, `-ink`, `-paper`, `-gradient`), each value, its
      contrast results, and **where each is used** (hero bg/text, logo, CTA, OG). This is T2's
      implementation contract.
- [ ] **Step 4:** Add a condensed palette swatch table to `BRAND.md` linking to the spec.
